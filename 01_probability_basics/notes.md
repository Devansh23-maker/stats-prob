## Mean (Expected Value)
The average value of a random variable.

For dice:
(1+2+3+4+5+6) / 6 = 3.5

---

## Variance
Measures how much values differ from the mean.

High variance = more spread  
Low variance = more stable  

---

## Standard Deviation
Square root of variance.  
It measures how much values typically deviate from the mean.

---

## Normal Distribution
Many real-world things follow a bell-shaped curve:
- Heights
- Marks
- Errors
- Prices

Mean = center  
Standard deviation = spread  

This is why Z-scores work.

---

## Why this matters for Data Science
Probability helps us:
- Handle uncertainty
- Model randomness
- Build machine learning
- Detect anomalies (Z-scores)

This is the foundation of AI and statistics.
